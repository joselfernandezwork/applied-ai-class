Tokenization: How AI Reads Text

The invisible first step that makes everything possible

Step 1 of 5: Tokenization
01
Step 1

It starts with your words

When you type a message to an AI, here's exactly what it receives:

"Hello, how are you today?"

→ The model doesn't "see" this the way you do. It needs to convert it.

02
Step 2 — Splitting into Tokens

Text gets sliced into "tokens" (word pieces)

Each token is roughly ¾ of a word. Punctuation often becomes its own token:

"Hello"
","
"how"
"are"
"you"
"today"
"?"

→ Each token is then assigned a unique numeric ID:

15496
11
4919
389
345
1909
30
💡 Scale check: GPT-4 processes up to 128,000 tokens at once. That's roughly a 96,000-word book — in a single conversation.
03
Step 3 — Embeddings (Meaning as Math)

Each token ID becomes a vector — a list of numbers that encode meaning

token "Hello" → [ 0.23, -0.45, 0.87, 0.12, -0.66, 0.34, 0.91, ... ] × 768 dimensions

↑ Each bar = one dimension of the embedding (768 total in GPT-2; up to 12,288 in GPT-4)

🔮 The magic: Words with similar meanings land near each other in this 768-dimensional space. "King" and "Queen" are close. "Dog" and "Cat" are close. "Dog" and "Democracy" are far apart.
04
Why This Matters for You

Practical implications of tokenization

💰
Tokens = Cost
Every AI API charges per token (input + output). A 10-page document ≈ 5,000 tokens. Know your context window limits.
✂️
Context Window = Memory Limit
If your conversation exceeds the limit, the AI "forgets" early parts. GPT-4: 128k. Claude: 200k. Gemini: 2M tokens.
🌐
Multilingual by Default
The same tokenizer handles 100+ languages. Each language uses different token counts — English is most efficient.
🔍
Powers Semantic Search
Embeddings enable "find documents about budget cuts" even when no doc says "budget cuts" exactly — meaning is matched, not keywords.

🎯 Key Takeaway: Every word you type is converted into numbers before the AI ever processes it. The quality of that number representation — the embedding — is what determines how well the AI understands nuance, context, and meaning. When you write clearer prompts, you give the model better signals to work with.
