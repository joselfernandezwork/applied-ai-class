01
The Fundamental Truth

LLMs are prediction machines — your prompt is the seed

🗑️
Vague prompt = Random output
The model fills gaps with the most statistically likely continuation — not what you actually need. Garbage in, garbage out. Applies to AI as much as any computer system.
🎯
Specific prompt = Targeted output
A well-structured prompt constrains the probability space to exactly what you want. Context, role, format, and tone each eliminate millions of possible wrong responses.
02
The CRAFT Framework

Five elements that transform any prompt

C
Context
Who you are, what situation you're in, background the AI needs
R
Role
What expert persona should the AI adopt? Senior analyst? Lawyer? Editor?
A
Action
The specific verb: Write / Analyze / Summarize / Compare / Generate / Debug
F
Format
Table / Bullets / Email / 3 paragraphs / JSON / Code / 200 words max
T
Tone
Formal / Casual / Persuasive / Technical / Executive-friendly

Real-World Before & After

Same task — completely different results

SCENARIO 1: Finance VP writing a board memo

❌ Weak Prompt
"Write me a report about Q1 results"
Missing: Who is this for? What numbers? What format? What level of detail? The AI has to guess everything and will produce something generic and useless.
✅ CRAFT Prompt
"You are a senior financial analyst at a $200M manufacturing firm. I am the CFO preparing a Q1 board memo. Write a 3-paragraph executive summary. Revenue was $48M (vs. $45M target). COGS increased 8% due to supply chain issues. Highlight 2 risks and 1 opportunity. Use formal, concise language. No bullet points."
Context: $200M firm, CFO Role: Senior analyst Action: Write exec summary Format: 3 paras, no bullets Tone: Formal, concise

SCENARIO 2: Sales leader preparing for a client meeting

❌ Weak Prompt
"Help me prep for a meeting"
The AI doesn't know the client, the deal size, the objections, or what "prep" means. It'll produce a generic agenda template that helps nobody.
✅ CRAFT Prompt
"You are an experienced enterprise sales coach. I'm a Sales Director at a SaaS company. I have a 30-min discovery call tomorrow with a VP of Operations at a 500-person logistics firm. Their main pain point is manual dispatch scheduling. I'm selling workflow automation software ($80k/yr). Give me 5 open-ended discovery questions, 2 anticipated objections + rebuttals, and one compelling value statement. Format as a coaching brief."

SCENARIO 3: Consulting firm building a strategy deck

❌ Weak Prompt
"Make me a PowerPoint about market entry strategy"
✅ CRAFT Prompt
"You are a McKinsey senior consultant. I need a 6-slide market entry strategy presentation for a private equity client evaluating entry into the U.S. healthcare SaaS market. Include: 1) Market size + growth, 2) Competitive landscape (top 5 players), 3) Entry modes (build/buy/partner), 4) Key risks, 5) Recommended entry path, 6) 100-day plan. Each slide should have a headline, 3 bullets, and one data point. Executive tone."
04
Advanced Techniques

Power user moves that multiply output quality

🔗
Chain-of-Thought: "Think step by step"
Adding this phrase dramatically improves reasoning accuracy. The model generates intermediate steps before the answer, catching errors it would otherwise miss.
"Analyze the profitability of this product line. Think step by step before giving your conclusion."
🪞
Ask It to Critique Its Own Output
LLMs get better when you ask them to review their own work. This activates a different reasoning mode and often catches logical gaps.
"Now review your previous answer. What are its 3 weakest points? How would you improve them?"
🎭
Persona Stacking
Combine two expert lenses for more nuanced analysis. This forces the model to reason from multiple frameworks simultaneously.
"You are both a healthcare regulatory attorney AND a product manager. Review this feature proposal for compliance risks and market fit."
📋
Give Examples (Few-Shot Prompting)
Show the AI exactly what format you want with 1–3 examples. This is one of the most reliable ways to get consistent, structured output.
"Here's the format I want:\nWeek 1: [Action] → [Result]\nWeek 2: [Action] → [Result]\n\nNow create a 4-week onboarding plan using this format."
🚫
Use Negative Constraints
Tell the AI what NOT to do. This is often more effective than only positive instructions for eliminating common AI failure modes.
"Do NOT use the word 'leverage'. Do NOT start with 'Certainly!'. Do NOT give generic advice — be specific to the pharma industry."

🎯 Key Takeaway: Prompting is a learnable skill — not a talent. The professionals who get 10× more out of AI tools are simply more specific, more intentional, and more iterative. Every conversation with AI is a collaboration: if the first response isn't what you need, add more context and ask again. The model isn't broken — your prompt needs tuning.
